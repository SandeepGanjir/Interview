## Rules

### Objective
Catch and evolve Pokemon to earn Trainer Points. **Be the first to reach 20 Trainer Points** to trigger the final round, then hold the highest total to be crowned the greatest Pokemon Trainer.

### Anatomy of a Card
Every Pokemon card has three major sections:
- **Pokemon:** the Pokemon sprite sits in the centre, with its name in the bottom-right corner.
- **Catch cost:** shown in the bottom-left as the types of Pokeball and the count of each required to catch it.
- **Top section:** made up of three areas:
   - **Trainer Points:** the Trainer Points the Pokemon is worth, shown at the far left.
   - **Discount:** the discount this Pokemon grants on future catches, shown at the top right as a count (1 or 2) and a Pokeball type. Starter and Tier 1–3 Pokemon give a 1-Pokeball discount; Rare, Legendary, and Mythical Pokemon give a 2-Pokeball discount. This mirrors how catching more Pokemon makes future catches easier in the anime.
   - **Evolution condition:** Tier 1 and Tier 2 cards show their evolution condition in the centre of the top section — a count and type of Pokeball discount needed to evolve, followed by an upward arrow to the next-stage Pokemon's sprite.

### Setup
1. **Set turn order:** decide the turn order (youngest trainer first, or at random), then hand each player the Player Card matching their position.
2. **Set up the board:** thoroughly shuffle each category of Pokemon cards. Deal 4 cards face-up into the empty slots for each of Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3, and 1 card face-up for the Rare and Legendary categories.
3. **Lay out tokens:** take out the trays matching your player count and place them beside the open book, within easy reach of every player. The number of each Pokeball type in the supply scales with the player count:
   - **2 players:** 4 of each Pokeball type, plus 4 Masterballs
   - **3 players:** 6 of each Pokeball type, plus 5 Masterballs
   - **4 players:** 7 of each Pokeball type, plus 5 Masterballs
4. **Optional — starting Pokemon:** going in reverse turn order, each player takes a Pokemon from the Starter category, either chosen or at random. All players must agree to choose; otherwise it's done randomly. You may skip the Starter mechanic entirely.

### On Your Turn
Players act in Player Card order, cycling around the table. Each turn has one mandatory action — exactly one of the three below — followed by an optional post-turn step.

- **Collect Pokeballs:** take either 2 tokens of the same type, or 3 tokens of different types, from Pokeball / Healball / Greatball / Friendball / Ultraball. You may not take 2 of the same type from a category that has 3 or fewer tokens left. If fewer than 3 types have tokens available, take as many different types as you can. *Holding limit:* you may not end your turn with more than 10 tokens (Masterballs included); return any excess to the supply.
- **Catch a Pokemon:** catch any Pokemon from the open cards or from your own reserved cards by paying its discounted cost back to the supply. *Discount mechanic:* each Pokemon you catch acts as a permanent Pokeball of the type shown in its top-right corner, lowering the cost of every future catch.
- **Reserve a Pokemon:** reserve one card from Tier 1, 2, or 3 only (no other categories), taken either from an open slot or from the top of that face-down deck, and keep it face-down in front of you. When you reserve, take 1 Masterball token if any are available — a Masterball is a wild card that stands in for any Pokeball type when catching. A reserved Pokemon grants no discounts or points while reserved, and you don't reveal it to other players. You may hold at most 3 reserved Pokemon, and cannot reserve more until one is freed up.

**Optional post-turn step:** refill any empty slots by dealing a new face-up card of the matching category. (If a category's face-down deck is empty, leave the slot empty.) Then you may evolve one of your Pokemon — see below.

### Evolving
You may evolve at most one Pokemon per turn, as your optional post-turn step, even if the condition was only met this turn. **Evolving costs no tokens.** To evolve:
- You must own Pokemons providing a discount equal to or greater than the Pokeball requirement shown in the card's top-centre.
- The next-stage card must be open on the board or held in your reserve.

Tuck the current Pokemon face-down under your Player Card and take the next-stage card in front of you. The next-stage card's higher Trainer Points and new discount now apply. *Note:* Pokemon tucked under the Player Card no longer provide discounts or Trainer Points.

### Mythical Pokemon (optional mechanic)
The moment you have exactly 3 Pokemon tucked under your Player Card (i.e. you've evolved exactly 3), claim a Mythical Pokemon — this is free and does not cost your action. Shuffle the Mythical deck, draw 1 card at random, and place it in front of you as though you'd just caught it. Each player may gain at most 1 Mythical Pokemon per game. Like a Legendary, it provides 2 Trainer Points and a 2-Pokeball discount of one type. You may skip the Mythical mechanic entirely.

### Game End & Winning
The round in which at least one player reaches 20 or more Trainer Points becomes the final round; the game ends once every player has completed that round so all players have had the same number of turns. Players then total their Trainer Points, and the highest total wins. Ties are broken in order by: most Pokemon evolved (cards tucked under the Player Card), then most Pokemon caught; if still tied, the players share victory.
